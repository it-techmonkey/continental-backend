iOS BUILD GUIDE - Testing on Friend's iPhone
============================================

PREREQUISITES:
1. Friend's iPhone UDID
2. Free Apple ID account (get at appleid.apple.com)
3. Xcode installed on your Mac

STEP 1: Get Friend's iPhone UDID
---------------------------------
Method A: On their iPhone
- Settings > General > About > Find UUID

Method B: Online (Easy!)
- Send this link to your friend:
  https://udid.tech
- They click "Get UDID" and share the code

Method C: iTunes/Finder (if they come to you)
- Connect iPhone to Mac
- Open Finder > Select their iPhone
- Click "Serial Number" (changes to UDID)

STEP 2: Update Bundle Identifier
---------------------------------
1. Open ios/Runner.xcodeproj in Xcode
2. Select "Runner" project
3. Under "TARGETS" > Runner > General
4. Change Bundle Identifier to something unique like:
   com.yourname.continental (replace yourname)
5. Save the project

STEP 3: Add Device to Your Apple ID
------------------------------------
1. Open Xcode
2. Preferences (Cmd + ,) > Accounts tab
3. Click "+" to add your Apple ID (if not added)
4. Select your Apple ID > Click "Manage Certificates"
5. Window > Devices and Simulators (Cmd + Shift + 2)
6. Click "+" > Enter device name and UDID
7. Select your Apple ID team
8. Done

STEP 4: Build the IPA
----------------------
In Terminal, run these commands:

cd /Users/manmohan/Desktop/chinmap/continental-app/App_continental
flutter build ipa --release

This creates: build/ios/ipa/continental.ipa

STEP 5: TestFlight (Best Option!)
----------------------------------
1. Create Apple Developer Account (FREE at developer.apple.com)
2. Go to App Store Connect
3. Create new app
4. Upload the IPA via Xcode or Transporter app
5. Add your friend to TestFlight
6. They download TestFlight app and test!

ALTERNATIVE: Direct Install (If same location)
----------------------------------------------
1. Send the IPA file to friend
2. They install via AirDrop, Email, or file sharing
3. Must trust developer profile on their iPhone
   Settings > General > VPN & Device Management > Trust

IMPORTANT NOTES:
----------------
- Free Apple ID allows testing on 10 devices max
- Ad-hoc builds expire in 90 days
- UDID must be registered before building
- iOS apps must be signed to install on real devices

TROUBLESHOOTING:
----------------
Error: "No valid code signing certificates"
Solution: Open Xcode > Preferences > Accounts > Download Manual Profiles

Error: "Developer mode disabled"
Solution: Settings > Privacy & Security > Developer Mode > Enable
